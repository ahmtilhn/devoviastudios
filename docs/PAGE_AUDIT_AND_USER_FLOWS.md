# Devovia Studio — Page Audit and User Flows

## Primary user flows

### 1. Discover a product

Homepage → Products → Product detail → Google Play or Support

User need: quickly understand what Devovia builds, inspect real proof and decide whether to install or learn more.

### 2. Start a project

Homepage/Services → Service context → Contact form → Confirmation

User need: understand fit, scope and next step without reading a long agency pitch.

### 3. Resolve a Google Play blocker

Services → Google Play Test Support → Process/FAQ → Request form

User need: identify whether Devovia understands the blocker and submit a focused request.

### 4. Get product support or privacy information

Product detail or Support hub → Product-specific policy/support link → Contact if needed

User need: find the correct product path without searching generic legal pages.

### 5. Validate studio credibility

Homepage/Products → Updates or Blog → Product detail/Contact

User need: see maintenance, expertise and real product activity.

## Page-by-page audit

### Homepage

Current strengths:

- Real product imagery.
- Clear product-studio positioning.
- Strong light visual direction.

Gaps:

- Motion system is not shared with subpages.
- Header/footer implementation differs from the rest of the site.
- Product proof and service explanation need a clearer narrative rhythm.

Target:

- Hero → capability proof → selected products → services → process → updates → about → CTA.
- One primary CTA: Explore products.
- Secondary CTA: Start a project.

### Products index

Gaps:

- Existing dark theme conflicts with homepage.
- Filters, cards and support card use different spacing and elevation rules.
- Product categories are visually equal even when relevance differs.

Target:

- Light page hero with product preview.
- Accessible filter pills with selected state.
- Consistent product cards with screenshot, category, outcome, metrics and CTA.
- Google Play support presented as a separate service card, not a product.

### Product detail

Gaps:

- Too many equally weighted panels.
- Screenshots, metrics, capabilities, privacy and release notes compete for attention.
- Gallery lacks a clear product story.

Target:

- Hero: product identity, outcome, metrics and primary install CTA.
- Sticky or staged screenshot narrative.
- Capabilities grouped by user outcome.
- Technical stack de-emphasized.
- Release, support and privacy grouped into a trust section.

### Services

Gaps:

- Cards explain categories but not outcomes, process or fit.
- Every service card has equal weight.

Target:

- Lead with outcomes and ideal client situations.
- Mobile app, game and web services use one shared card pattern.
- Google Play support remains visually distinct because it solves an urgent blocker.

### Google Play Test Support

Gaps:

- Dense two-column layout creates early form pressure.
- Process, problems, FAQ and deliverables compete.

Target:

- Problem recognition first.
- Four-step process second.
- Deliverables and FAQ through progressive disclosure.
- Request form after trust is established; sticky only on wide screens.

### Updates

Gaps:

- Timeline and product summaries use different visual systems.
- Filters dominate before the user understands the page.

Target:

- Clear chronological timeline.
- Product filter as a secondary control.
- Each update shows outcome, date, product and next action.

### Support and privacy

Gaps:

- Product links, request types, privacy shortcuts and FAQ appear as separate systems.
- The correct next step is not always obvious.

Target:

- Start by selecting a product or request type.
- Show the most common actions first: support, privacy, billing, release notes.
- Keep the request form secondary until a path is selected.

### Blog index

Gaps:

- All cards carry similar visual weight.
- Categories are numerous relative to the amount of content.

Target:

- One featured article, then a quieter article grid.
- Fewer visible categories; secondary categories can be progressively disclosed.
- Clear reading time, topic and outcome.

### Blog article

Gaps:

- The article body lacks reading rhythm and in-page structure.
- CTA appears only at the end.

Target:

- Narrow reading column.
- Sticky metadata or compact table of contents on desktop.
- Clear heading rhythm, callouts and related article links.
- Contextual CTA that matches the article topic.

### Contact

Gaps:

- The form begins without setting expectations.
- No visible response time, process or privacy summary near submission.

Target:

- Short positioning statement and expected response time.
- Group fields into contact, project and context.
- Persistent labels and clear status feedback.
- Trust note beside the submit action.

## Consistency requirements

Every route must share:

- Sora/Manrope typography.
- Light canvas and white surfaces.
- The same header and footer treatment.
- The same button hierarchy.
- The same section rhythm and card radii.
- The same focus states and form behavior.
- The same scroll-reveal and reduced-motion behavior.
- The same content hierarchy: context → proof → detail → action.
