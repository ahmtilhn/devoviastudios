5. Purposes for Using Data

Data may be processed for the following purposes:

  • to provide the application’s core stock and inventory features;
  • to create accounts, enable sign-in, verify email addresses, and keep accounts secure;
  • to allow users to manage product, warehouse, stock, customer, supplier, report, document, and backup records;
  • to deliver premium, subscription, ad removal, and Business entitlements;
  • to verify and restore purchases and subscriptions and prevent abuse;
  • to display ads, run rewarded ads, measure ad performance, and manage advertising preferences;
  • to provide product information suggestions through artificial intelligence/OCR;
  • to send notifications, low-stock alerts, reminders, or in-app status messages;
  • to provide user support and respond to requests;
  • to detect crashes, performance issues, and security problems;
  • to prevent fraud, unauthorized access, abuse, and policy violations;
  • to comply with legal obligations and protect our rights;
  • to perform export, sharing, printing, or opening-in-another-application actions explicitly initiated by the user.

6. Legal Bases

Depending on applicable law, data may be processed under one or more of the following legal bases:

  • establishment and performance of a contract;
  • the user’s explicit consent;
  • taking action at the user’s request;
  • compliance with legal obligations;
  • legitimate interests of Devovia or the user;
  • security, fraud prevention, and protection of service integrity;
  • establishment, exercise, or defense of legal claims.

For processing based on consent, the user may withdraw consent through the relevant feature or platform settings. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out based on consent before its withdrawal.

7. Third-Party Service Providers

The application may work with service providers in the following categories to operate its features:

  • account creation, sign-in, and email verification services;
  • cloud backup, data storage, and server processing services;
  • in-app purchase and subscription management services;
  • advertising network, ad mediation, ad measurement, and consent management services;
  • artificial intelligence, OCR, text recognition, and barcode reading services;
  • crash, performance, security, and abuse prevention services;
  • file sharing, printing, email, messaging, or destination applications selected by the user;
  • authorized authorities and professional advisors where legally required.

These providers are subject to their own privacy policies, terms of service, and country/region-based rules. Devovia does not have full control over third-party providers’ independent account decisions, system outages, data center locations, advertising decisions, or policy changes.

8. Data Sharing

Devovia does not sell users’ local inventory records. Data may only be shared for the purposes described in this policy, upon the user’s instruction, with service providers, application stores, advertising/measurement providers, cloud infrastructure providers, support providers, authorized authorities where legally required, or destination applications explicitly selected by the user.

In some countries, personalized advertising or cross-app/cross-site measurement within advertising technologies may be considered “sale,” “sharing,” or “targeted advertising.” The user may manage their preferences through the relevant platform privacy settings, in-app privacy options, device advertising settings, or, where applicable, mechanisms such as “Do Not Sell or Share / Opt-out.”
