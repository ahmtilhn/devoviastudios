2.4 Images, Documents, and Files

Product photos, invoice or document images, product documents, PDF outputs, CSV/Excel/JSON exports, backup files, application logos, and other files selected by the user may be processed. These files may be stored on the device, exported, shared, or associated with the user’s account if the cloud backup feature is enabled, depending on the user’s preferences.

2.5 Camera, Photo, and File Selection

Camera permission may be used for barcode/QR scanning, taking product photos, or adding document images. Photo and file selection may be used to add an image or document explicitly selected by the user to the application. The application does not use these permissions to scan all personal files on the device independently of the user.

2.6 Artificial Intelligence and OCR Product Reading Data

The user may optionally scan text, barcodes, or product information from product photos. This feature may generate suggestions such as product name, brand, variation, color, size, quantity, weight, volume, price, expiration date, lot number, and description. AI or OCR results are suggestions only; the user must review the information before saving it.

When this feature is used, selected product images and the text extracted from those images may be processed to fulfill the request, prevent abuse, manage quality, security, and usage limits. If the user does not use this feature, product images are not processed for this purpose.

2.7 Purchase, Subscription, and Entitlement Information

For in-app purchases or subscription features such as ad removal, Premium+, and Business, product ID, purchase status, subscription status, transaction ID, store verification information, entitlement status, and refund/cancellation/renewal status may be processed. Full payment card numbers, bank accounts, or payment passwords are not viewed by Devovia; payments are managed by the relevant application store.

2.8 Advertising, Measurement, and Marketing Data

In free or ad-supported use, ad impressions, ad clicks, rewarded ad preferences, ad performance information, device or advertising identifiers, consent status, app interactions, and purchase events may be processed. Ads may be personalized or non-personalized depending on the user’s consent status, device settings, country, and platform policies.

2.9 Technical, Security, and Performance Data

Device model, operating system, application version, language, country/region, IP address, crash logs, performance information, network diagnostic information, and security and abuse prevention signals may be processed. This data is used to keep the application secure, functional, and up to date.

2.10 Support and Communication Data

If the user submits a support request, the email address, message content, attached files, screenshots, and information required to resolve the request may be processed.

3. Where Do We Obtain Data From?

Data may be obtained directly from the user, the user’s device, files selected by the user, account providers, application stores, advertising and measurement providers, cloud services, artificial intelligence/OCR services, support communications, and sharing/export actions explicitly initiated by the user.

4. Local Storage, Cloud Backup, and Synchronization

The application may store core inventory data on the device. Unless the user initiates an export, sharing, printing, or backup process, local content may remain on the user’s device.

If Business or cloud backup features are used, inventory data, product images, document files, report files, PDF outputs, application settings, and backup records may be stored in cloud infrastructure by associating them with the user’s account. This feature is provided so that the user can restore their data on a new device or reduce the risk of data loss in case of device loss.

Use of cloud backup may be subject to limitations, technical interruptions, storage limits, and third-party service provider terms. Users are advised to perform regular exports and independent backups for critical business records.
