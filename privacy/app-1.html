What the app may process

The exact scope depends on the features used, permissions granted and services enabled on the device.

Business and account context

Inventory workflows may include names, email details, business information or other records entered by the user.

  • Business and workspace details
  • User-entered contact information
  • Settings required to operate the inventory workflow

Products and transactions

The app can process the records users create to manage stock and understand inventory activity.

  • Products, SKU, barcode and category data
  • Stock levels, purchases, sales and movement history
  • Prices, quantities, supplier and invoice context

Device capabilities

Optional permissions support visible features and can be controlled through Android settings.

  • Camera access for barcode or QR scanning
  • File or media access for product images and exports
  • Internet access for eligible updates, support or synchronization features

Diagnostics and reliability

Technical information may be used to understand crashes, performance issues and compatibility problems where applicable.

  • Device and app version information
  • Crash or diagnostic events
  • Performance and reliability signals

Your choices, retention and support

Privacy choices remain connected to the feature that needs them.

Permission control

Camera, media and related permissions can be reviewed or withdrawn through Android settings. Some features may no longer work after a permission is disabled.

Retention and deletion

Locally stored records remain on the device until the user edits, deletes, resets or removes them through available app or device controls. Backup files remain under the user’s control.

Access and privacy requests

Questions involving privacy, access or deletion can be sent directly to Devovia Studio.
